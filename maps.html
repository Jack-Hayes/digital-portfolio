Maps

It's weird how I ended up in synthetic aperture radar and scientific programming all because I took an "Introduction to GIS" course in undergrad. I just liked maps.

Most of my mapmaking lately involves products from high-resolution DEMs (a lot of my inspiration comes from the WA DNR "Bare Earth" hillshades) trying to capture the topography of different features on Earth. Mostly volcanoes, if I'm being honest. I grew up on ArcPro some years ago, but I've started to realize I can pull off the same things with QGIS and free image editing software. If Gerardus Mercator managed with ink and paper, I figure I can handle it without a bajillion-dollar license.

I wish I had more to share here from my efforts in conservation planning and mapping racial gerrymandering, but this page is sparse for now.

Volcanoes

Universidad de Colima, Colima Intercambio e Investigación en Vulcanología

In loose collaboration with the Universidad de Colima's University Center for Volcanological Studies, I develop hazard maps for the estimated effects of the 1913 VEI-4 Sub-Plinian Eruption of Volcán de Colima on modern day infrastructure and population.
